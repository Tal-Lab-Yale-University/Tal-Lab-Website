Abigail (Abby) Solomon is a Postgraduate Research Associate beginning
her third year in the Tal Lab. Before joining the lab, Abby graduated
magna cum laude with a Bachelor of Science (BS) in Biology and a minor
in Chemistry from Salve Regina University in Newport, Rhode Island.
During her time at Salve, she spent three years in a microbiology lab
investigating *Salmonella enterica* serovar Typhimurium and its role in
plant-pathogen interactions.

Since joining the Tal Lab in 2024, Abby has worked on a variety of
projects to investigate progenitor cells and their contribution to
uterine injury and repair. Additionally, Abby helped develop and
optimize a septum resection surgery to improve the reproductive
outcomes of laboratory mice affected by longitudinal vaginal septum.
Her work was recognized by the Society for Reproductive Investigation
(SRI) as an Infographic Contest Winner in 2025 and was recently
published in *Reproduction and Fertility* in 2026. For the next year,
Abby's work will focus on collecting and culturing tissue biopsies from
women in the postpartum period to investigate the fibrotic and
senescent capacities of endometrial stromal cells.

When she's not in the lab, Abby enjoys spending time with her family
and friends on the lake, traveling, reading, and tending to her
tropical plant garden.
