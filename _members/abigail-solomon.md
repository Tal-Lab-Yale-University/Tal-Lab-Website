Abigail (Abby) Solomon is a Postgraduate Research Associate beginning
her third year in the Tal Lab. Since joining in 2024, Abby has worked
on a variety of projects to investigate mesenchymal progenitor cells
and their contribution to uterine injury and repair utilizing
transgenic mice as well as primary human endometrial cell culture
models. Additionally, Abby helped develop and optimize a septum
resection surgery technique to improve the reproductive outcomes of
laboratory mice affected by longitudinal vaginal septum. Her work was
recognized by the Society for Reproductive Investigation (SRI) as an
Infographic Contest Winner in 2025 and was recently published in
*Reproduction and Fertility* in 2026.

Before joining the lab, Abby graduated magna cum laude with a Bachelor
of Science (BS) in Biology with a minor in Chemistry from Salve Regina
University in Newport, Rhode Island. During her time at Salve, she
spent three years in a microbiology lab investigating *Salmonella
enterica* serovar Typhimurium and its role in plant-pathogen
interactions.

When she's not in the lab, Abby enjoys spending time with her family
and friends on the lake, traveling, reading, and tending to her
tropical plant garden.
