Pranav graduated with a BS in Computer Science and Mathematics from
Dickinson College in May 2025. His undergraduate research involved
understanding the gene expression profile of the PMA response in the AML
cell line HL-60.

Besides his research at Dickinson, Pranav has extensive research
experience at Oregon Health and Science University (OHSU) and Yale
University. At OHSU, Pranav worked on extending and improving a
submodular feature-based algorithm to select subsets of scRNA-seq
atlases while preserving the transcriptional heterogeneity within the
data. At Yale, prior to joining the Tal Lab, Pranav spent a year in the
lab of Dr. Markus Muschen, where he gained experience in the analysis of
genomic, proteomic, and transcriptomic data modalities such as ChIP-seq
and Hi-C, as well as genome-wide association studies (GWAS), to
understand the biology of B-cell malignancies.

In the Tal Lab, Pranav hopes to expand his understanding of postpartum
endometrial remodeling and computational techniques for analyzing
spatial and single-cell transcriptomic datasets before attending
graduate school.

In his free time, Pranav likes to play pickleball, dance salsa, and
play hard rock solos on his electric guitar.
