Sydney Bell is a Postgraduate Associate in the Tal Lab at Yale University. She graduated from the University of Texas at Austin with High Honors, earning a Bachelor of Science and Arts (B.S.A.) in Human Ecology and Departmental Honors. During her undergraduate studies, Sydney conducted clinical and public health research focused on improving adult and prenatal vaccination rates through implementation science and clinical transformation initiatives.

Since joining the Tal Lab in 2024, Sydney has investigated the cellular and molecular mechanisms that regulate uterine injury, repair, and fibrosis using both experimental and computational approaches. Her work has included analyzing large-scale single-cell and bulk RNA sequencing datasets to characterize immune and stromal cell populations involved in postpartum uterine remodeling and endometrial fibrosis, as well as studying signaling pathways that contribute to fibrotic remodeling in human endometrial stromal cells.

Outside of the lab, she enjoys crafting, traveling, and spending time with family and friends.
